## 🏥 PART I: MEDTECH INNOVATION FRAMEWORKS

### 1. BIODESIGN PROCESS (Stanford Methodology)

**Three-Stage Innovation Process:**

#### STAGE 1: IDENTIFY
- **1.1 Strategic Focus** - Define areas of interest, market segments
- **1.2 Needs Exploration** - Observe clinical environments, identify unmet needs
- **1.3 Need Statement Development** - Articulate specific, validated needs

#### STAGE 2: INVENT
- **2.1 Disease State Fundamentals** - Deep clinical understanding
- **2.2 Existing Solutions** - Competitive landscape analysis
- **2.3 Stakeholder Analysis** - Map all players (patients, providers, payors)
- **2.4 Market Analysis** - Size, growth, dynamics
- **2.5 Needs Selection** - Prioritize based on strategic fit

#### STAGE 3: IMPLEMENT
- **3.1 Concept Generation** - Ideation and brainstorming
- **3.2 Initial Concept Selection** - Screen and filter ideas
- **3.3 IP Strategy** - Patent and protection planning
- **3.4 R&D Strategy** - Technical development roadmap
- **3.5 Clinical Strategy** - Trial design and evidence generation
- **3.6 Regulatory Strategy** - FDA/CE/market approval pathways
- **3.7 Quality Management** - ISO, QSR compliance
- **3.8 Reimbursement Strategy** - Coding, coverage, payment
- **3.9 Marketing & Sales Strategy** - Go-to-market planning

**Key Principles:**
- Global perspective (Africa, China, Europe, India, Japan, Latin America)
- Value-conscious healthcare (cost-effective innovation)
- 70+ case studies of real medtech innovations
- Transdisciplinary approach (engineers + physicians + business)

### 2. LEAN CUSTOMER DEVELOPMENT

**Core Philosophy:**
- "Get out of the building" - Direct customer contact
- Hypothesis-driven experimentation
- Validate before building
- Continuous learning loops

**Key Frameworks:**

#### Customer Development Process
1. **Customer Discovery** - Test problem hypotheses
2. **Customer Validation** - Test solution hypotheses
3. **Customer Creation** - Scale user base
4. **Company Building** - Transition to execution

#### Validated Learning
- Formulate falsifiable hypotheses
- Design experiments to test them
- Measure actual customer behavior (not just what they say)
- Pivot or persevere based on evidence

#### MVP (Minimum Viable Product) Strategy
- Build smallest thing to test hypothesis
- Measure meaningful metrics
- Learn what customers actually do
- Iterate rapidly

**Key Questions to Answer:**
- Who is the customer?
- What is their urgent problem?
- How are they solving it today?
- What would make them switch?
- How much would they pay?

### 4. SIX SIGMA FOR MEDTECH

**What is Six Sigma?**
- Statistical measure: 99.9997% defect-free (3.4 defects per million)
- Business philosophy: Continuous improvement focused on customer needs
- Methodology: DMAIC process for improvement

**DMAIC Framework:**

| Phase | Activity | Key Tools |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| **D**efine | Define problem, project scope, customer requirements | Charter, SIPOC, CTQ Tree |
| **M**easure | Measure current process performance | Data collection, MSA, Process Sigma |
| **A**nalyze | Analyze data to find root causes | Cause & Effect, FMEA, Hypothesis Testing |
| **I**mprove | Develop and implement solutions | DOE, Brainstorming, Pilot testing |
| **C**ontrol | Control the improved process | Control Charts, Process Management |

**Key Tools for MedTech:**
- **SIPOC**: Suppliers, Inputs, Process, Outputs, Customers
- **CTQ Tree**: Critical To Quality characteristics
- **FMEA**: Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (essential for device risk)
- **Control Charts**: Monitor process stability
- **Pareto Charts**: Focus on vital few causes
- **Process Sigma**: Quantify process capability

**Process Capability Levels:**
- 0-2 Sigma: Too much variation, hard to meet specs
- 2-4.5 Sigma: Moderate, most output meets requirements
- 4.5-6 Sigma: Very little variation, virtually all output meets requirements

## 💬 PART II: COMMUNICATION FRAMEWORKS

### 5. NLP (NEURO-LINGUISTIC PROGRAMMING) FOR INFLUENCE

**Core Model:**
- **Neuro**: Neurology and five senses
- **Linguistic**: Language for describing and communicating
- **Programming**: Habitual patterns of thoughts, feelings, actions

**Key Techniques:**

#### Building Physical Rapport
- Mirroring and matching body position, posture, movement, rhythm, breathing
- Matching voice tone, speed, volume, rhythm
- Do NOT mimic - be subtle

#### Building Verbal Rapport
- **Representational Systems**: Visual, Auditory, Kinesthetic, Olfactory, Gustatory
- **Predicates**: Language patterns that reveal representational systems
  - Visual: "I see what you mean", "We see eye to eye"
  - Auditory: "Loud and clear", "Word for word"
  - Kinesthetic: "I can grasp that idea", "I got the hang of it"

#### Milton Model (Indirect Hypnosis)
- Artfully vague language patterns
- Allows listener to fill in meaning
- Useful for influence and persuasion

**NLP Presuppositions:**
1. The map is not the territory
2. Experience has a structure
3. Mind and body are parts of the same system
4. If one person can do something, anyone can learn it
5. People already have all the resources they need
6. You cannot NOT communicate
7. The meaning of your communication is the response you get
8. Underlying every behavior is a positive intention
9. People are always making the best choices available to them
10. If what you're doing isn't working, do something else

### 7. CRUCIAL CONVERSATIONS

**Definition:** Conversations where stakes are high, opinions vary, emotions run strong

**Key Frameworks:**

#### Start with Heart
- Focus on what you really want
- Refuse "fool's choices" (either/or thinking)
- Work on "me" first

#### Learn to Look
- Watch for when conversation turns crucial
- Notice safety problems (people feeling unsafe)
- Watch for violence or silence

#### Make It Safe
- Step out of content when needed
- Rebuild mutual purpose
- Use contrasting (don't/do statements)
- Apologize when appropriate

#### Master My Stories
- Separate facts from stories
- Watch for "victim, villain, helpless" narratives
- Tell the rest of the story

#### STATE My Path
- **S**hare your facts
- **T**ell your story
- **A**sk for others' paths
- **T**alk tentatively
- **E**ncourage testing

#### Explore Others' Paths
- Be curious
- Ask questions
- Mirror (reflect emotions)
- Paraphrase
- Prime (suggest what they might be thinking)

#### Move to Action
- Decide how to decide
- Document decisions
- Assign responsibilities
- Set follow-up

### 8. THE 7 HABITS OF HIGHLY EFFECTIVE PEOPLE

**Character Ethic vs. Personality Ethic:**
- Character ethic: Integrity, humility, fidelity, courage, justice, patience (foundational)
- Personality ethic: Techniques, quick fixes, social band-aids (superficial)

**The 7 Habits:**

#### PRIVATE VICTORY (Independence)
1. **Be Proactive** - Take responsibility for your choices
2. **Begin with the End in Mind** - Define your mission, values, goals
3. **Put First Things First** - Prioritize based on importance, not urgency

#### PUBLIC VICTORY (Interdependence)
4. **Think Win/Win** - Seek mutual benefit
5. **Seek First to Understand, Then to Be Understood** - Empathic listening
6. **Synergize** - Value differences, create 3rd alternatives

#### RENEWAL
7. **Sharpen the Saw** - Continuous self-renewal (physical, mental, spiritual, social)

**Key Concepts:**
- **Inside-Out**: Change starts with self
- **Paradigm Shifts**: See differently to act differently
- **Circle of Influence**: Focus on what you can control
- **Emotional Bank Account**: Build trust through deposits
- **P/PC Balance**: Production vs. Production Capability

### Stakeholder Mapping (Integrated)

**Primary Stakeholders in MedTech:**
1. **Patients** - End users, outcome recipients
2. **Physicians** - Prescribers, operators
3. **Providers** - Hospitals, clinics
4. **Payors** - Insurance, government
5. **Regulators** - FDA, CE, notified bodies
6. **Investors** - VCs, strategics
7. **Partners** - Suppliers, distributors

**Mapping Dimensions:**
- Influence (high/low)
- Interest (high/low)
- Attitude (supportive/neutral/resistant)
- Information needs
- Communication preferences

### Communication Strategy for MedTech

#### For Clinical Audiences
- Use evidence-based language
- Reference clinical studies, trials
- Respect time constraints
- Focus on patient outcomes
- Use visual aids (anatomy, flowcharts)

#### For Business Audiences
- Lead with market opportunity
- Use financial metrics (NPV, IRR, TAM)
- Address risk mitigation
- Show competitive differentiation
- Include regulatory pathway clarity

#### For Technical Audiences
- Provide detailed specifications
- Include engineering rationale
- Reference standards (ISO, IEC)
- Show testing data
- Address interoperability

#### For Regulatory Audiences
- Follow submission guidelines precisely
- Use established terminology
- Provide comprehensive risk analysis
- Show equivalence or clinical evidence
- Demonstrate QMS compliance
