## 🔬 PART III: NEUROVASCULAR SPECIFIC CONSIDERATIONS

### Cerebral Aneurysms
- **Ruptured aneurysms**: Emergency clipping/coiling within 24-72 hours
- **Antiplatelet management**: Dual antiplatelet for stent-assisted coiling
- **Triple-H therapy**: Hypertension, hypervolemia, hemodilution (controversial)
- **Vasospasm monitoring**: TCD, clinical exam, angiography
- **Rebleeding prevention**: Early definitive treatment

### Arteriovenous Malformations (AVMs)
- **Spetzler-Martin grading**: Size, eloquence, venous drainage
- **Hemorrhage risk**: 2-4% annually for unruptured
- **Management options**: Surgery, radiosurgery, embolization, observation
- **Preoperative embolization**: Reduces intraoperative bleeding
- **Normal perfusion pressure breakthrough**: Postoperative edema/hemorrhage

### Cavernous Malformations
- **Hemorrhage risk**: 0.5-25% depending on location and history
- **Surgical indications**: Symptomatic, accessible, progressive deficits
- **Seizure management**: Often indication for surgery
- **Familial forms**: Multiple lesions, genetic counseling

### Moyamoya Disease
- **Revascularization**: Direct (STA-MCA) vs. indirect (EDAS, EMS)
- **Perioperative stroke risk**: Highest in first 7 days
- **Blood pressure management**: Critical for preventing stroke
- **Antiplatelet therapy**: Standard postoperative management

### Carotid Stenosis
- **Symptomatic vs. asymptomatic**: Different thresholds for intervention
- **CEA vs. CAS**: Endarterectomy vs. stenting
- **Antiplatelet protocols**: Aspirin + clopidogrel for CAS
- **Perioperative MI risk**: Cardiac monitoring

### Vertebral Artery Dissection
- **Anticoagulation vs. antiplatelet**: Controversial
- **Endovascular stenting**: For pseudoaneurysm or recurrent ischemia
- **Posterior circulation stroke**: Higher morbidity

### Anticoagulation Reversal Decision Matrix

| Agent | Reversal Strategy | Onset | Duration | Notes |
|-------|------------------|-------|----------|-------|
| **Warfarin** | Vitamin K + PCC/FFP | 4-6h (PCC) | Variable | INR goal <1.4 |
| **Dabigatran** | Idarucizumab | Minutes | 24h | DTT normalizes |
| **Rivaroxaban/Apixaban** | Andexanet alfa | Minutes | 2h | Factor Xa specific |
| **Heparin** | Protamine | Minutes | 2h | 1mg per 100 units |
| **LMWH** | Protamine (partial) | Minutes | 8h | Not fully reversible |
| **Aspirin** | Platelets + DDAVP | 1-2h | 24h | Irreversible effect |
| **Clopidogrel** | Platelets + DDAVP | 1-2h | 5-7d | Irreversible effect |

## 🎯 KEY TAKEAWAYS

### For Emergency Neurosurgery:
1. **Time is brain** - Rapid decompression for mass lesions
2. **ATLS first** - Stabilize before definitive neurosurgical intervention
3. **ICP management** - Prevention of secondary injury
4. **Multidisciplinary approach** - Neurosurgery, neurocritical care, anesthesia

### For Bleeding/Coagulation:
1. **Know your patient's medications** - Anticoagulants are common
2. **Have reversal strategies ready** - Know dosing and availability
3. **Balance thrombosis vs. bleeding** - Both are dangerous in neurosurgery
4. **Use evidence-based protocols** - But individualize to patient

### For Neurovascular Disease:
1. **Prevent rebleeding** - Early intervention for ruptured lesions
2. **Monitor for vasospasm** - Days 3-14 are critical
3. **Antiplatelet management** - Critical for endovascular procedures
4. **Blood pressure control** - Essential for preventing complications
